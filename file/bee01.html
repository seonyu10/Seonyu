Introduction

BeeCanvas

Files, Images, Links, Youtube, SoundClound. Collect and organize everything visually in one place. BeeCanvas is a visual collaboration tool that lets you share and comment a image, links, or other multimedia resources on a canvas whatever you want.

Challenges

How to promote BeeCanvas?

It was important to understand when and why people have difficulty doing creative work and convince people to use BeeCanvas. For the better understanding, we needed to do the user research finding out what kind of difficulties and their needs. We also had to search the limit of our competitor and our strength to them.

Situation Analysis 01 : User research

Interviews with potential customers

To undestand how actually creative people work and what kind of productivity tool they use in their fields was extremly important. Through deep interviews with creative people in various fields, I found some common difficulties when sharing their rough ideas with people and organizing various types of file. They had the strong need for shared knowledge. It was a common challenge when a group of intelligent people gathers to solve a complex or difficult problem. How can they see the whole picture and understand what the problem is?

Situation Analysis 02 : Competitor analysis

Research on productivity tools

I had to investigate existing productivity tools on the market. There are many kinds of collaboration tools, such as file sharing tools, instant messaging tools, cloud storaging tools, and visual collaboration tool. BeeCanvas is a visual collaboration tool based on interactive whiteboard, among other similar competitors: realtimeboard, mural, milanote. Among these many existing collaboration tools, it was important to position where the BeeCanvas was located and how competitive BeeCanvas would be in the market compared to other services.

Marketing Strategy 01 :

Market positioning

Comparison with No.1 Competitor for viral marketing

After identifying out potential customer’s needs for visually organizing stuff and research about our competitor, I came up with an idea that it would be effective to explain BeeCanvas by comparing with Evernote, which is the No.1 competitor in productivity tool. Rather than comparing with the similar visual collaboration tool like Realtimeboard, Mural or Milanote and analyze the subtle diffrerences between them, I thought it would be more persuasive to compare BeeCanvas with Evernote. I thought positioning BeeCanvas as the opposite tool of Evernote in term of creative thinking process and managing creative projects would work for viral marketing.

Post on Brunch(Korean version of Medium)

Evernote was the most well-known productivity app which has the opposite user experience of BeeCanvas. I wrote an article about why I began using BeeCanvas as a user. I showed an example of my Evernote which was used for ideations about city branding commercial with my teammates. We gathered various types of media, like youtube videos, web links, pdf files, music files. It was not easily to make connections between things and organize this information at a glance on Evernote.

Sharing ideas and posting documents from the web became much more fun on BeeCanvas and it was easier to make comments on the post-its that the other teammates posted.

Providing the actual use case of my own, I explained BeeCanvas is useful in terms of creative thinking process and collaboration. Creative thinking is an inherently messy, non-linear process. In a traditional note-taking app like Evernote, text, links, and images quickly wind up a disorganized mess, and moving items around with cut and paste is cumbersome.

I pinpoint that unlike Evernote, users can gain efficiency by making the process visible on the canvas. BeeCanvas helps others to 'get inside our heads' in order to convey a thematic setting for a design or to explain function in piece of work.—if someone can put pen to paper and make an idea tangible in the moment, the group can have an informed opinion, react, and iterate on the fly. BeeCanvas is a free-form note-taking application designed to bring order to the chaos.

Market Positioning - Outcome

Succesful viral marketing

We saw positive results— 30,000 views and 1,000 shares of the article via SNS and Facebook! Comparison with Evernote was effective to make people understand BeeCanvas is for visual creative projects. Offering actual usecases were helpful as well as people can imagine how it would be useful to use BeeCanvas.

Ads on Facebook

Market Positioning - Landing page

An Eye-catching Tagline

The tagline is a phrase that describes our product in one word, and it was a very important issue in marketing. Comparing Beecanvas to evernote, we conveyed that Beecanvas is a visual collaboration tool. Based on this positioning, I wanted to convey that BeeCanvas helps to boost people's creativity. So I suggested a tagline of BeeCanvas, which is ”We know your ideas are more than just words.” I also designed the landing page with the tagline with the image that supports the tagline well.

Marketing Strategy 02 :

Target Marketing

Provide real-world examples

Based on the user research that how our customers use BeeCanvas, i showed several use cases, which made it possible to imagine for potential users how they can use in their creative work. Because the way people actually use is different for each purposes, it was very important to provide appropriate examples and use for each target when marketing.
