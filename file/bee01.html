Introduction

BeeCanvas

Files, Images, Links, Youtube, SoundClound. Collect and organize everything visually in one place. BeeCanvas is a visual collaboration tool that lets you share and comment a image, links, or other multimedia resources on a canvas whatever you want.

Challenges

How to promote BeeCanvas?

It was important to fully appreciate the challenges people face during their creative work. For deeper understanding, we needed to carry out some user research into the obstacles they encounter and their likely needs. We also had to consider our competitors and our strengths and weaknesses when compared to them.

Image from IDEO

Situation Analysis 01 : User research

Interviews with potential customers

Gaining an understanding about how creative people actually work, and the kind of productivity tools they use in the field, was crucial. Through in-depth interviews with creatives across many spheres, I found a commonly-mentioned difficulty to be the sharing of rough ideas and organizing various types of files. A clear need for efficient knowledge-sharing was identified. It is a universal challenge for groups of intelligent people collaborating on complex or difficult projects. How can all stakeholders get to see the bigger picture and better understand what the problems might be?

Situation Analysis 02 : Competitor analysis

Research on productivity tools

I had to investigate the existing productivity tools on the market. There are many kinds; tools for file sharing, instant messaging, cloud storage, and visual collaboration. BeeCanvas is itself a visual collaboration platform based on an interactive whiteboard, with some similar competitors like realtimeboard, mural, milanote. Among these existing collaboration tools, it was important to position BeeCanvas strategically and ensure it could have a competitive edge in the marketplace.

Marketing Strategy 01 :

Market positioning

Comparison with No.1 Competitor for viral marketing

After identifying out potential customers' needs for visual organization, and researching our competitors, I decided that explaining BeeCanvas by comparing it to Evernote, the No.1 name in productivity tools, would be most effective. I felt this was preferable and more persuasive than contrasting it with similar visual collaboration offerings like Realtimeboard, Mural or Milanote, and highlighting the subtle differences between them. Positioning BeeCanvas as an opposite alternative to Evernote, from the perspective of creative thinking and the management of creative projects, offered a lot of potential scope for viral marketing.

Post on Brunch(Korean version of Medium)

Evernote was the best-known productivity app with a noticeably different user experience to BeeCanvas. I wrote an article explaining what drew me to BeeCanvas as a user. I showed an example from my Evernote which I had collaborated on with my teammates for ideas regarding city branding commercials. We had collated various media files, like youtube videos, web links, pdf files, and music. It had not been easy to make connections between these disparate components and organize everything at a glance on Evernote.

Sharing ideas and posting documents from the web became much more fun on BeeCanvas and it was easier to make comments on the post-its that the other teammates posted.

Using this experience as a basis, I explained how BeeCanvas is useful in terms of the creative thinking process and team-working. This approach to working is an inherently messy, non-linear process and in a traditional note-taking app like Evernote, text, links, and images quickly get heaped in a disorganized jumble, and moving things around with cut and paste is cumbersome.

I highlighted how, unlike with Evernote, BeeCanvas users enjoy greater efficiency by having their process visible on the entire canvas. It helps others involved to 'get into our heads', to illustrate a thematic setting for a design, or to explain function in a piece of work. If someone can put pen to paper and manifest their idea in the moment, the broader group can then form an informed opinion, react, and iterate on the fly. BeeCanvas is, therefore, a free-form note-taking application designed to bring order to the chaos.

Market Positioning - Outcome

Succesful viral marketing

We saw positive results — 30,000 views and 1,000 shares of the article via SNS and Facebook! The comparison with Evernote was very effective to help people understand quickly how BeeCanvas is a tool for visual creative projects. Offering genuine use case histories was helpful also, because it made it easier to imagine the useful ways in which BeeCanvas could be applied.

Ads on Facebook

Market Positioning - Landing page

An Eye-catching Tagline

Taglines are succinct catchy phrases that capture a product in one word, and it's a very important factor in marketing. We conveyed the idea that BeeCanvas is a visual collaboration tool like Evernote but beyond there, I wanted to highlight how it helps to boost creativity also. So I suggested the following tagline:

”We know your ideas are more than just words.”

I also designed the landing page incorporating the phrase, with an image to complement it nicely.

Marketing Strategy 02 :

Target Marketing

Provide real-world examples

Based on user research into how our customers use BeeCanvas, I showed several use cases, allowing potential users to visualize how they could use it in their creative work. Because people use these tools in diverse ways depending on their purpose, it was very important in the marketing to provide appropriate examples of uses and applicability.
